About

With over seven years of experience as an engineer at the Industrial Technology Research Institute (ITRI), Taiwan, I have developed and applied artificial intelligence and computer vision techniques to various projects, ranging from biomedical to aerospace domains. My work includes several years of hands-on experience with Dynamic Vision Sensors (DVS), advancing their application in ultra-high-speed object detection for challenging real-world scenarios.

Selected Projects

Knee Joint Moment Prediction

Deep learning with IMU data and reinforcement learning for injury risk assessment.

Dynamic Vision Sensors

Event-camera pipeline for ultra-fast objects and stereo fusion, leveraging Dynamic Vision Sensors (DVS) for ultra-high-speed object detection, real-time object avoidance, and precision capture. Developed and applied various event-data processing methods that highlight the advantages of DVS technology, including microsecond-level temporal resolution for extreme speeds and high dynamic range performance in challenging lighting conditions.

Exercise and Physiology in Isolated, Confined, and Extreme Environments

Multidisciplinary collaboration with Hydronaut and Czech Technical University (CTU) to design and implement a comprehensive monitoring system for physiological parameters during exercise in Isolated, Confined, and Extreme (ICE) environments. This work, conducted under the Czech Delta-2 project, integrates sensing technologies, data analytics, and human factors research to support analog space mission applications, enabling real-time assessment and optimization of crew health and performance..
